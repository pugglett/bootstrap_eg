Images with Bootstrap

You must add class="img-responsive" to each of your <img> tags.

The img-responsive class allows your images to resize themselves according to whatever amount of the page they take up.

Image size matters!

Larger images are able to resize much nicer than smaller images - if your pictures look blurry, it's probably because the source image is too small. However, file size matters too - if your images are large and your page is taking a long time to load, look into compressing those images so that they load faster.

Image shape matters!

Bootstrap provides some nice CSS to change how your images look - if you want your image to be a circle but it's displaying as an oval, it's likely because your image is not a square.
